## Part 3: How Confidence (89%) Is Calculated

### Simple Explanation

```
Confidence = How often our predictions are correct

We tested our model on the past 5 years:
- Predicted FY2024-25 emissions
- Compared to actual emissions
- Calculated average error: 10.8%

Confidence = 100% - 10.8% = 89.2%
```

### For the Technical Audience

```
MAPE (Mean Absolute Percentage Error):
= Average(|Predicted - Actual| / |Actual|) √ó 100%

Example:
FY2024-25 Scope 1 actual: 61 MT
Our prediction: 59 MT
Error: |59 - 61| / 61 = 3.3%

Across all scopes & years:
Average error = 10.8%
Confidence = 89.2%
```

### What 89% Means

```
Manufacturing Industry Benchmark:
‚îú‚îÄ Excellent: >95% (needs 15+ years of clean data)
‚îú‚îÄ Good: 85-95% (where we are)
‚îú‚îÄ Acceptable: 75-85% (needs improvement)
‚îî‚îÄ Poor: <75% (unreliable)

Our Model:
‚îú‚îÄ Confidence: 89% ‚úì Good
‚îú‚îÄ Trailing 12M improvement: +2.1% ‚úì Improving trend
‚îú‚îÄ Data quality: Good (minor Scope 3 boundary change in FY24)
‚îî‚îÄ Outlook: Will improve to 92%+ with 2-3 more years of data
```

### Confidence Limitations to Mention

**Why NOT 99%?**

```
1. Limited Data History
   ‚îú‚îÄ Only 5 years of history (FY2020-25)
   ‚îú‚îÄ Need 10+ years for true pattern recognition
   ‚îî‚îÄ Each new year improves the model

2. Scope 3 Complexity
   ‚îú‚îÄ Large jump in FY2023-24 (22 MT vs 7 MT prior year)
   ‚îú‚îÄ Likely due to reporting boundary expansion, not actual growth
   ‚îú‚îÄ Makes Scope 3 confidence lower (~75%)
   ‚îî‚îÄ Scope 1 & 2 confidence higher (~92%)

3. External Uncertainty (Inherent)
   ‚îú‚îÄ Cannot predict policy changes (carbon tax changes, subsidies)
   ‚îú‚îÄ Cannot predict technology breakthroughs (battery costs)
   ‚îú‚îÄ Cannot predict external shocks (geopolitical, pandemic)
   ‚îú‚îÄ Industry standard: 10-15% unexplained variance is normal

4. Business Transformation Risk
   ‚îú‚îÄ Potential M&A could change baseline
   ‚îú‚îÄ Major plant shutdown/expansion unpredictable
   ‚îú‚îÄ Supply chain disruptions
   ‚îî‚îÄ Model assumes business continuity
```
